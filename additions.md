## JEJU ISLAND — Near Parnas Hotel Jeju (Seogwipo)

### 🏔️ Hiking & Nature

#### Hallasan (한라산) — South Korea's Highest Peak
Only two trails reach the summit (Baengnokdam crater):

| Trail | Distance | Time | Notes |
|-------|----------|------|-------|
| **Seongpanak (성판악)** | 9.7km one-way | 6-8 hrs | Most popular, gradual incline, reservation required |
| **Gwaneumsa (관음사)** | 8.7km one-way | 8-10 hrs | More scenic but steeper, reservation required |

**2025 Update:** New shuttle buses from Jeju Intercity Terminal. Book reservations at [KNPS Website](https://www.knps.or.kr) — slots fill fast.

#### Seongsan Ilchulbong (성산일출봉) — Sunrise Peak
UNESCO World Natural Heritage. Dramatic tuff cone rising 182m from the sea.

- **Distance:** 1.1 km round trip (~30-50 min to summit)
- **Fee:** ₩5,000
- **Tip:** Arrive 5-6 AM for sunrise, fewer crowds
- **Nearby:** Seopjikoji coastal trail, 1.3km easy walk

**[Kakao Map: 성산일출봉](https://map.kakao.com/link/search/성산일출봉)**

#### Bijarim Forest (비자림숲)
Ancient gotjawal (lava forest) with 300+ year-old castor-aralia trees. Hidden gem, very peaceful.

**[Kakao Map: 비자림숲](https://map.kakao.com/link/search/비자림숲)**

#### Jeju Olle Trail — Eastern Routes
- **Course 1** — Siheung to Gwangchigi: 9.3 mi, stunning coastal
- **Course 3B** — Onpyeong to Pyoseon: passes Seongsan Ilchulbong area

### Drinking Rules
1. **Cash** — Most traditional spots are cash-only
2. **Follow locals** — Look for ajusshis in suits
3. **No menu?** — Ask prices first
4. **Rounds (차)** — Dinner → pocha → drinking (typical order of a night out)
5. **Don't pour your own** — Pour for others, they'll pour for you
6. **With seniors** — Turn away when drinking (sign of respect)
7. **Weekends** — Venues more crowded, especiallyFri-Sat nights
8. **Weekdays** — Better for local-only spots
